The Grand Hotel experience as intimate paper artifact. The player is not looking at a game screen -- they are handling evidence. Guest registers, telegrams, typed case notes, hand-annotated maps, and hotel stationery with monograms. The lobby is not a room to photograph. It is a place described through the documents you hold.

Lobby Visual Direction

The lobby as archive -- a working reception desk where every surface holds paper evidence.

The lobby is not rendered as a scene. Instead, the player encounters it through artifacts: a concierge's sketch, a guest register entry, a bellhop's note about Room 14, and a floor plan with pencil annotations marking "irregular activity."

Details

Typography Feel

Monospaced typewriter for case notes and interface text. Elegant serif for hotel stationery, letterheads, and formal names. Occasional italic for handwritten annotations. The contrast between typed and handwritten creates intimacy and personality.

Materials & Textures

Foxed paper, carbon copies, manila folders, wax seals, postal stamps, typewriter ribbon, hotel letterhead, pressed blotting paper, ruled ledger lines. Every surface is a document surface.

Ornament Level

Ornament comes from the artifacts themselves: hotel monograms, embossed crests, telegram borders, postal markings, and the ruled lines of a guest register. No decorative UI chrome -- the documents are the decoration.

What This Challenges

Every existing board presents the Grand Hotel as a visual environment -- a room to look at, with a game UI layered on top. This board argues that the strongest mystery experience might not show the hotel at all. Instead, the player holds the hotel's paper trail: its registers, its telegrams, its private correspondence. The interface IS the evidence. This challenges the assumption that immersion requires a rendered scene, and proposes that intimacy with physical artifacts -- the feeling of rifling through someone's private papers -- can be more immersive than any lobby photograph.

Best For

A Grand Hotel mystery that feels like opening a private case file. Best if the project should prioritize narrative intimacy, textual discovery, and the pleasure of reading between the lines. This direction turns every clue into a tangible object and every interaction into an act of investigation rather than observation. Strongest option for players who love reading, puzzles, and the feeling of holding evidence in their hands.
