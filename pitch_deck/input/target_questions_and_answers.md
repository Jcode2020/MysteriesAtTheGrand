# Pitch Deck

Maximum: `5 slides`

Goal: prove this could become a real business.

This is for inspiration only. You can structure your slides as you wish. Just remember:

- `2 minutes` for the pitch
- `2 minutes` for the demo
- `2 minutes` for Q&A

| Who has this problem? Be specific: `"B2B sales teams"`, not `"businesses"`. | Premium and above-market hotels, especially luxury hotels, that need to sell an experience, not just a bed. This is especially true in the luxury hotel market, where differentiation comes from story, identity, and emotional resonance. Supporting quote from Christoph Hoffmann / 25hours: "bevor wir uns hinsetzen und ein Hotel entwickeln, brauchen wir eine Geschichte" and "jedes Hotel muss seine eigene Geschichte haben." Source: Bain & Company, [Global luxury spending to land near EUR 1.5 trillion in 2024](https://www.bain.com/about/media-center/press-releases/2024/global-luxury-spending-to-land-near-1.5-trillion-in-2024-remaining-relatively-flat-as-consumers-prioritize-experiences-over-products-amid-uncertainty/). |
| How big is this market? Include number of companies, users, or spend.       | Using the star-comparable hotel market as the cleanest global proxy, DELTA CHECK identifies `386,144` star-rated hotel properties worldwide. DELTA CHECK also states that `Europe = 50%` of global star-rated hotels, which implies `193,072` star-rated hotel properties in geographic Europe. In the current hybrid model in `pitch_deck/input/hotel_market_segmentation_worldwide.md`, the target `premium + luxury` segment is about `93,864` hotels worldwide and about `49,507` hotels in geographic Europe. A more conservative Europe-only framing is `41,104` premium + luxury hotels on the current EU exact basis (`4,606` luxury + `36,498` premium), with the caveat that the Europe star split is still marked as best-effort / provisional. The visual summary for this is already prepared in `pitch_deck/input/hotel_market_segmentation_chart.svg`, showing `Worldwide`, `World without Europe`, and `Europe`. |

| Note: investors fund markets, not features.                                 | Pitch takeaway: even with a narrow, star-based segmentation, this is not a niche with only a few hundred buyers. The current working view is roughly `94k` premium + luxury hotels worldwide, including about `50k` in geographic Europe on the best-effort estimate, and about `41k` on the stricter EU exact basis. That is enough to frame this as a real B2B category, not just a creative demo. Source logic and caveats are documented in `pitch_deck/input/hotel_market_segmentation_worldwide.md`. |
